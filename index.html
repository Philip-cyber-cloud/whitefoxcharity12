The Woman

A Girl from
Rheinberg
Who Changed Fashion

Born on August 25, 1970 in Rheinberg, Germany, Claudia Schiffer was discovered at a disco in Düsseldorf at the age of 17. Within months, she had transformed from a law student into the most photographed woman on the planet.

She became the face of Chanel under Karl Lagerfeld, the global ambassador for Guess?, and a cultural phenomenon who graced the covers of Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, Elle, and Cosmopolitan more than any other model in history.

Often called the “last real supermodel,” Claudia redefined what it meant to be a top model — combining classic German beauty with unmatched professionalism, intelligence, and grace.

The Journey

A Life in Fashion & Film

1987

Discovered in Düsseldorf

Spotted at a disco at age 17 by a model scout. Within a year she moved to Paris and signed with Metropolitan Models, launching one of the greatest careers in fashion history.

1989

The Face of Guess?

Her iconic black-and-white Guess? jeans campaign made her a household name globally, echoing Brigitte Bardot and cementing her as the definitive blonde bombshell of her generation.

1992

Chanel & Karl Lagerfeld

Became the exclusive muse of Karl Lagerfeld at Chanel — a partnership that defined 1990s high fashion. She walked virtually every Chanel show and campaign throughout the decade.

1994

The Original Supermodel Era

Alongside Naomi Campbell, Linda Evangelista, Cindy Crawford, and Christy Turlington, Claudia defined the “supermodel” phenomenon — the first models to become genuine celebrities.

2003

Love Actually & Film Career

Appeared in the beloved British romantic comedy “Love Actually,” playing herself in a memorable cameo alongside Colin Firth, proving her charm extended far beyond the runway.

2010s

Businesswoman & Brand

Launched her own fashion line, authored books on style and photography, and became a respected voice in fashion as a designer, curator, and industry mentor.

Today

Enduring Icon

At over 50, Claudia continues to appear on magazine covers, attend major fashion events, and inspire millions worldwide. Her legacy as one of the greatest supermodels is unquestioned.
